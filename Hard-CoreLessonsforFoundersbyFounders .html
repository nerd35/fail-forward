Hard-Core Lessons for Founders by Founders



On Saturday, June 29th, budding startup founders and stakeholders in the Nigerian tech ecosystem came together to discuss, the failure stories of their startup ventures and what we could learn from them.

It was an emotional and intensely insightful episode with speakers and members of the audience sharing freely what went wrong, dealing with the feeling of failure & the invaluable and priceless lessons from the experience.

There is a prevalent narrative amongst entrepreneurs which associates failure as an event with the individual who failed. This often leads to negative emotions and discouragements. Some founders have been known to give up after such experience or even venture to take their own lives due to the pain of the demise.

Fail Forward seeks to change this narrative through entrepreneurship education and creating a platform where established founders can share practical and insightful lessons from their failure experience with other startup entrepreneurs in the tech ecosystem.







Hence, in her second event, Fail Forward hosted renowned speakers including Onyeka Akumah, Founder and CEO FarmCrowdy, Femi Iromini, Co-founder & CEO of Turbo and Sheriff Shittu, Founder and CEO, GetDev.co. Sheriff Shittu shared about how he ran an online furniture business that failed primarily due to depending on third party vendors for quality furniture and timely delivery. Many clients complained about the low quality and poor service which eventually led to the shutdown of the business. He detailed his comeback and how he was able to evolve into GetDev and leverage on the failure lessons from previous business to fail forward.

Onyeka Akumah shared how QuickGist, a news aggregating platform, became successful within days of launch and got $50,000 investment. In 6 months, he was bankrupt and had to use his house rent and savings to pay off staff salaries. He shared how he turned around that failure, repositioned his business as a website development, management and advertising platform and became successful.

Some failure lessons shared at the event are: ensure your business idea is in an industry you have experience in, pick the right co-founders and team and don't do a business based on "hoped revenue."

Other lessons gleaned include: be observant about opportunities because they are everywhere, have an exit plan for your business(es) and if you meet your goals for the business, you can sell or shut down





Fail Forward is billed to hold again in the fourth quarter of the year 2019 as it is a quarterly event. In addition to hosting veteran startup founders as speakers, its programme includes a Failure lessons session for the audience to share their own experiences and lessons. In future editions, it would also include the #FailureTest, a pitch where founders pitch some of their plans and veterans identify possible failure points and proffer solutions.

Asides from hosting events and curating Failure Stories, Fail Forward has evolved into a global tech community where we incubate tech businesses and help them to succeed globally.
